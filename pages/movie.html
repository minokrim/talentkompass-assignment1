American Psycho" is a psychological thriller film directed by Mary Harron, based on the novel of the same name by Bret Easton Ellis. Released in 2000, the film stars Christian Bale as Patrick Bateman, a wealthy investment banker in Manhattan who leads a secret life as a serial killer. The movie is known for its dark humor, satirical take on the excesses of 1980s yuppie culture, and its exploration of the thin line between sanity and madness. Christian Bale's performance as the charismatic yet deeply disturbed Patrick Bateman is widely praised for its intensity and complexity. American Psycho has gained a cult following for its unique blend of horror, satire, and social commentary. The film's controversial themes and surreal narrative have sparked discussions about the nature of identity, consumerism, and the consequences of unchecked ambition. Whether appreciated for its sharp social critique or criticized for its explicit content, "American Psycho" remains a provocative and memorable cinematic experience for those drawn to its unconventional storytelling and psychological depth.
